The Colosseum

The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is an ancient Roman gladiatorial arena in Rome, Italy. It was constructed in AD 70-80 under Emperor Vespasian and could hold over 50,000 spectators.

The Colosseum was used for gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and theatrical performances. Today, it is one of the most visited landmarks in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
