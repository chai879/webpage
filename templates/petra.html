Petra - The Lost City

Petra, often called the "Rose City" due to its pink sandstone cliffs, is an ancient city in Jordan. It was the capital of the Nabataean Kingdom and a major trading hub around 300 BCE.

Petra is famous for its intricate rock-cut architecture, including the iconic Treasury (Al-Khazneh) and the Monastery (Ad Deir). The city was later abandoned and rediscovered in 1812 by Swiss explorer Johann Ludwig Burckhardt.
