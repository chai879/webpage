Christ the Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer (Cristo Redentor) is a colossal statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Completed in 1931, it stands 98 feet (30 meters) tall, with an arm span of 92 feet (28 meters), and is perched atop Mount Corcovado.

The statue was designed by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa and sculpted by French artist Paul Landowski. It symbolizes peace and Christianity and is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.
