Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Incan citadel located in the Andes Mountains of Peru. It was built by the Inca emperor Pachacuti and later abandoned during the Spanish conquest.

The site remained hidden from the outside world until it was rediscovered in 1911 by the explorer Hiram Bingham. Today, Machu Picchu is recognized as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
