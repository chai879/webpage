Chichén Itzá

Chichén Itzá is a large pre-Columbian archaeological site built by the Maya civilization. It is located in the Yucatán Peninsula, Mexico. This iconic site served as a major political, economic, and religious center between the 7th and 10th centuries.

One of its most famous structures is the Temple of Kukulcán, also known as "El Castillo," a pyramid that aligns with the equinoxes to create a shadow illusion of a serpent descending.

Opening Hours: 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM (Daily)
